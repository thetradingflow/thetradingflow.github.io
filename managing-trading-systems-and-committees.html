Managing trading systems and committees

The signal_generator module primarily works with trading systems.

Trading systems can be constructed in many ways, from top-down or bottom-up approaches:

  • a cross-sectional strategy of N instruments produces 2N trading systems (N long, N short)
  • a time-series strategy of N instruments produces 2N trading systems
  • a long/short binary technical trading system (eg, dual moving average) produces 2 trading systems
  • a long/short continuous trading rule produces 2 trading systems
  • a long-only tactical asset allocation model on N instruments produces N trading systems
  • …

Some trading systems are called inter-market systems, others are market-specific systems, some are binary, others are continuous, etc.

Committees combine trading systems in optimal ways, but do not care nor need to know how trading systems are implemented.

In my framework, a class called TradingSystemData abstracts away their implementation details, and contains all the relevant data for a trading system that is needed by a committee to make its allocation decisions:

  • the instrument it trades
  • the time series of its positions and pnls
  • the list of its trades
