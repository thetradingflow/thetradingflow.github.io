A trading system skeleton

A trading system (TS) is defined by 3 components:

  • an entry rule
  • a sizing rule
  • an exit rule

A TS is always:

  • unidirectional: long-only or short-only
  • applied to a single asset
  • executed at no cost at the current bar’s closing price (ie, no delay)

From this skeleton I can construct two families of trading systems:

  1. Binary trading systems (BTS): a BTS enters into a position when its entry rule is triggered, and exits it when its exit rule is triggered. The position size is held constant during the life of the trade. Entry rule triggers while a position is on are skipped.
  2. Continuous trading systems (CTS): contrary to a BTS, a CTS updates its position size and exit rule every time its entry rule is triggered. A CTS effectively removes the path-dependency associated with BTS at the cost of a potentially higher turnover.

These TS are very flexible:

  • a cross-sectional trading system is a collection of individual trading systems
  • a buy-and-hold strategy is a long-only unlevered trading system with an entry triggered on the first bar and an exit rule which never triggers
  • a long/short trading system is the combination of two TS, one long and one short
