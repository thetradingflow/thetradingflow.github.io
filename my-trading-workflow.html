My trading workflow

My trading workflow is composed of 4 main modules, each with a clear set of responsibilities:

  • market_data: updates the market data database. It extracts forex and futures data and metadata from IB Gateway and updates the production database.
  • account_data: updates the account data database. It extracts the account’s capital, P&L, orders, transactions, and positions from the broker. It reconciles these values against internal calculations for accuracy.
  • signal_generator: ingests market data and produces a model portfolio, ie a target weight for each continuous futures in its universe.
  • order_engine: ingests the model portfolio, market and account data and produces a set of orders to submit to the broker. These orders include roll and rebalancing orders for the following trading day.

Each module is executed at the end of each trading day after markets are closed at 4:15 pm EST.

Whenever a critical event is trigerred by any of the 4 modules, I’m alerted on my phone and the workflow execution is paused until I manually resume it. Otherwise each module produces a report for the given day that I can quickly go through to see if anything out of the ordinary happened. Orders are then submitted automatically to the broker.

In the future, I plan on adding a couple of modules:

  • execution_engine: an event-driven program whose responsibility will be to improve the execution of the orders and provide information about the quality of execution.
  • reporting_engine: generates additional reports for monitoring the health of the system (eg, rolling statistics on portfolio data, accounting/tax liability, market liquidity, performance of individual models, IT stuff like memory/cpu usage of the server, etc.).
